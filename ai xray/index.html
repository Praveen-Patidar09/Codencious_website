THE CHALLENGE Why Facial X-Rays Often Miss the Mark Even the slightest shift in head position during a facial X-ray can render the image unusable.
This forces technicians to repeat scans — increasing radiation exposure, delaying diagnoses,
and raising costs for both patients and providers.
Repeat Scans → More radiation exposure
Specialized Hardware → Costly and hard to scale
Manual Adjustments → Time-consuming and inconsistent
Up to 30% of facial X-rays need to be retaken due to misalignment — increasing risk and cost.
